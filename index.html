The Opportunity
A Style That's Pulling in New Shoppers
Mucho Mas showed that shoppers want a sweeter, fruitier, easier-drinking Spanish Red — +67% growth in a year. These are people who find traditional Rioja too serious. El Rico speaks directly to them: same approachability, but with a genuine Rioja story behind it.
The Timing
A Rule Change That Just Opened the Door
Rioja DOCa has just allowed higher residual sugar for the first time. The brand is registered and ready. Mack & Schuhle carry it into Germany. First in gets to define what this style means to shoppers — that window is open right now.
The Positioning
Rioja Made Easy — Without Dumbing It Down
Target £7.50–8.50 — just above Mucho Mas (£7.22) to signal something more interesting, without putting off shoppers who'd never normally reach for Rioja. Rich, smooth, fruit-forward. A wine that says yes before you've opened it.

The Winemaker
Norrel Robertson MW
El Rico is being made by Norrel Robertson — one of around 400 Masters of Wine worldwide and a specialist in Spanish wine. He's working with Vivanco in Rioja, one of the most respected houses in the region. The combination of his technical skill, Vivanco's track record, and the new DOCa approval gives El Rico a story that's genuine, easy to tell, and hard to copy — the kind a shop assistant can share in ten seconds, and a shopper remembers next time they're in the aisle.
